A. About the Contest

Mayor Art S.A.C. (the "Organization") announces the first international photography competition "I was at Machu Picchu"/ "I was in Machu Picchu (the " Contest "), which shall be governed by the following Terms.

Participation in the Contest implies acceptance of these Rules in their entirety.

B. Participation

Who may participate?

The contest is open to the general public regardless of nationality or age at time of entry. Participation is open to contestants, voters and Professional Photographers.

Employees and contractors of Quimera Holding Group or any of its subsidiaries and affiliates (or co-organizers, sponsors, suppliers and auditors of the Contest) and their immediate family (spouse (s), parents, children, siblings) are not allowed to participate in the Contest.

Ways to participate

· Entering photos in the contest

· Voting for participating photos.

To participate by entering photos.

The Participant must register for the Contest by completing the registration form on machu-picchu100.com indicating their name, surname(s), email, password, gender, date of birth and contry of residence. The Organization will send a confirmation email once the photo is accepted to participate in the contest. Once registered, the Participant may upload up to 5 photos.
The competition is divided into 10 stages with a duration of 7 days each. Each participant may submit up to 5 images per Voting Period, each photo placed in the appropriate Contest Categor (see Categories). The 5 photos may participate in a single Category or in 5 different Categories.


Once the Voting Period begins, the number of votes will be visible only to the Participant on their profile page and through emails to be sent by the Organization. The final number of votes each photo receives will be visible once published by the Organization.

To participate by voting for photos.

Voting Partiipants must first register at machu-picchu100.com and then vote for up to five images per Voting Period (participating in any Category), which at its discretion, consider the best among those that have been uploaded by the Participants, without distinction according to categories.

After each stage of voting will be awarded to 10 voters per drawing (see Awards). Each of the 5 votes adds a chance of winning the drawing.

C. Contest Mechanics

C.1. Categories

Each photo entered in the contest must be placed in one of the 7 Contest Categories (the “Categories”). The Contest Categores are:

1. People. Photographs of people inside the tourist circuit.

2. Landscapes and Nature. Landscapes, nature, animals.

3. En Route to Machu PIcchu. Fotografías tomadas en el viaje a Machu Picchu (camino Inca, tren, etc.).

4. Mystical. Fotografías relacionadas con la el misticismo y la experiencia mística de Machu Picchu.

5. Architecture. Photographs of any of the buildings of the citadel, such as the Temple of the Sun, the Intihuatana, etc.

6. Fun. Creative and fun photographs taken inside the circuit.

7. Vintage. Photographs taken before 1970.

Each photo may only be placed in one Category. In the event that a photograph is placed in a Category that the Organizers determine is inappropriate, the Organizers reserve the right to change the Category in which the photo is placed at any time up until the voting begins (see Contest Calendar).

C.3. Mechanics

After each Voting Period, the 10 photos with the most votes (without regard to category) will advance to the Grand Final. The photos that are not finalists will be automatically entered in the next Voting Period with a score of 0. In the event of a tie for tenth place, all entries tied for 10th place will advance to the Grand Final.

C.4. Grand Final

After each Voting Period, the 10 photos with the most votes (without regard to category) will advance to the Grand Final.

A jury appointed by the Organization will evaluate the images and choose 7 winners (one per category), among which a Grand Prize winner will be chosen. The Judges will also rank the winners in the following order: Grand Prize Winner, 2nd Place Winner, 3rd Place Winner, 4th Place Winner, 5th Place Winner, 6th Place Winner and 7th Place Winner. During the celebration of the Grand Final, the jury will also elect seven (7) Alternate (runner-up) winners in teh following rank order: 1st Alternate Winner, 2nd Alternate Winner, 3rd Alternate Winner, 4th Alternate Winner, 5th Alternate Winner, 6th Alternate Winner and 7th Alternate Winner with the aim of ensuring that if the Grand Winner and / or some winners do not meet the requirement to provide information necessary to emit the prizes, the prizes are made available to alternate winners in the rank order provided in the Grand Final.

This election will be held on August 22, 2011 at 10am GMT-005 (Lima), at the offices of Mayor Art SAC, Via Km 1.5 Uchumayo Variant - Arequipa - Arequipa - Sachaca, in the presence of the Notary Public and Representative of the Directorate General of Government Affairs, for the purpose of recording the Grand Winner, Winners and ranking positions. The results will be published the same day on the website (www.machu-picchu100.com). Winners will be notified via email. Jury decisions are final and irrevocable.

C.5. Judges

C.5.1. Judges for Category Entries

The jury will consist of 8 persons chosen by the Organization, among whom will be designated a Chairman, who will award prizes. In case of a tie, the Jury President shall have the casting vote.

The jury must choose among the 100 pictures (or more in the case of a tie) who passed to the Grand Final, in a single round of reviews for each category, the 7 Winners (one per category). If one or more categories have no entries, the jury must elect in a second round, the number of Winners necessary to complete the group of 7 winners. In this second round, the Winners will be elected without regard to Category.

Among the 7 winners, the Jury will elect in a single round the Grand Prize Winner and the rank order of the 7 winners (Grand Prize Winner, 2nd Place Winner, 3rd Place Winner, 4th Place Winner, 5th Place Winner, 6th Place Winner and 7t Place Winner) and the 7 runnner-up alternate winners (1st Alternate Winner, 2nd Alternate Winner, Winner 3rd Alternate, 4th Alternate Winner, 5th Alternate Winner, 6th Alternate Winner and 7th Alternate Winner).

In the event of a tie, the Jury President shall have the casting vote.

Each stage of the competition will be subject to supervision and control of the representative appointed by the Directorate General of the Peruvian Ministry of the Interior, who will decree the announcement of winners compliance with accountability for effective delivery of prizes.

D. Contest Conditions

D.1. Photography

Photos must be taken in the Machu Picchu tourist circuit or en route to Machu Picchu.

Photographs must not infringe on any copyrights or constitute fraud. Photographs submitted to the competition must not, according to the criterion of the Organization, contain obscene images, provocative, defamatory, sexually explicit or inappropriate content, or to affect the cultural heritage or morality.

With regard to digital editing and alteration of images will be accepted according to the criteria detailed in paragraph D.1 in the Official Rules.

The Organization reserves the right to verify the validity and originality of the pictures and data of the participants, and to disqualify any participant who fails to comply with any provision of the Official Rules.

The following edits are permitted:

Minimal darkroom editing is permitted to achieve the desired sharpness and color balance. Your photos must be authentic, however, and we reserve the right to reject photos that have been digitally altered. Please use the following guidelines when editing your photos:

· Minimal dodging (to brighten shadows) and burning (to darken highlights) is permitted in order to achieve an image that closely resembles what you saw. Drastic changes to saturation are not permitted.

· Use of Fish Eye Lenses

· Stitched panoramas (created from several photos taken at different angles from the same location and at the same time and using the same focal length) are permitted only if all photos were taken within the same timeframe. All stitched images must use the same focal length. Please indicate in your photo caption that the image is stitched. If it is not noted as such, we reserve the right to disqualify the photo.

· High Dynamic Range Images (HDRI) are permitted if needed to more accurately portray the range of luminance that you witnessed. HDRI images are permitted only if taken at the same time, from the same location and using the same focal length. If it is discovered that your photos were taken at different times (more than a few seconds apart), we reserve the right to disqualify the photo. Please indicate in your photo caption that the photo was processed using HDRI. If your HDRI image is submitted without notation in your caption, we reserve the right to disqualify the photo.

The following edits are not permitted:

· Outright digital alterations are not permitted. Your shot may be rejected if it is discovered that you have digitally added or removed any part of the image.

· Drastic changes to saturation and exposure are not permitted.

· Digital filters are not permitted. Your photo will be rejected if your photo uses the automatic alteration filters in your digital photo software (solarization, mezzotint, duotone, etc.).
